Overview

Abstract

Cellular senescence plays a fundamental role in aging, tissue homeostasis, and disease progression. However, predicting senescence states from spatial transcriptomics imaging data remains challenging due to the lack of standardized benchmarks and domain-specific computational approaches.

We present XenSen-Bench, the first benchmark for evaluating senescence prediction methods on 10X Xenium spatial transcriptomics cell images. Our benchmark includes two tissue types — normal human colon and ovarian cancer — with multi-scale cell images (2.5x, 5.0x, 10.0x) and DeepScence-derived senescence scores. We propose a domain-specific approach combining Masked Autoencoder (MAE) pretraining on unlabeled cell images with multi-scale fusion, demonstrating that domain-specific foundation models significantly outperform general-purpose pretrained models (ImageNet, DINOv2) for this task.

Approach

Method

Two-stage approach: domain-specific self-supervised pretraining followed by supervised multi-scale fusion for per-cell-type senescence prediction.

1

MAE Pretraining

Self-supervised Masked Autoencoder pretraining on 685K+ unlabeled cell images.

  • ViT-Small encoder (384-dim, 12 layers)
  • 75% random patch masking
  • Scale-specific pretraining per context level
  • Learns domain-specific visual representations
2

Multi-Scale Fusion

Separate pretrained encoders per scale, features fused for prediction.

  • Scale-specific MAE-pretrained encoders
  • CLS token concatenation & fusion
  • Per-cell-type regression heads
  • Layer-wise learning rate decay
XenSen-Bench pipeline: MAE pretraining on unlabeled cell images (left) and multi-scale fusion with scale-specific encoders (right).
